European Emigration to America (1620-1650)

BASICS

  1. Who migrated?
    • 2.6 million Europeans migrated from Europe to the Americas
      • This consisted of people of religious beliefs, farmers, poor and rich, people of all sorts came to “the new world.”
      • 90% of British Settlers
        • For example, 20,000 Puritans arrived in the region between 1630 and 1640 in search of a place to freely practice their religion.
  2. Was this a voluntary or forced migration?
    • It was both forced and voluntary.
      • Forced because they were looking for ways to expand their beliefs but were also in search of religious freedom.
      • Voluntary because they wanted to increase their wealth through greater economic opportunities such as farming and better jobs.

REASONS WHY THEY MIGRATED

  1. Why did these people migrate?
    • They migrated to seek religious freedom while getting economic opportunities through farming.
  2. Was this migration politically, socially, or economically motivated?
    • Both Socially and Economically
      • Socially because of religious persecution
      • Economically because of economic opportunities
        • They were persecuted because of religion and were told to go to the outskirts of Holland but economic stability wasn’t good there so with the permission of King Charles if they were permitted to go to North Eastern America to settle and farm. They were funded by the London Virginia Company. In return the settler had to give a large portion of their crops grown in the New World to the Company.
